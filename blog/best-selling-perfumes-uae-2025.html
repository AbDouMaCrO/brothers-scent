25 Best-Selling Perfumes in the UAE in 2025 (Wholesale Buyer's List)

The fragrances moving fastest off UAE retail shelves right now — with wholesale margin notes to help you stock smarter.

BrotherScents July 18, 2025 9 min read

If you're a perfume retailer in the UAE, Kuwait, or Saudi Arabia, knowing which fragrances to stock first is everything. Wrong SKU choices tie up capital in slow-moving inventory; the right picks can turn over your initial investment in weeks. This list is compiled from real-world sales data across the GCC retail sector, cross-referenced against our wholesale movement volumes at BrotherScents. These are not recommendations based on online reviews — these are the fragrances UAE consumers are actually buying, and that retailers keep reordering.

We've split the list into three categories: Affordable Best-Sellers (the volume drivers), Designer Classics (the trust builders), and Niche Risers (the margin maximizers). Every retailer should have a blend of all three.

Category 1 — Affordable Best-Sellers (High Volume, High Margin)

These are the workhorses. They move fast, they have repeat buyers, and they deliver the kind of margin that keeps your business healthy. Stock deep on these.

01
Armaf Club De Nuit Intense Man

The UAE's single best-selling affordable fragrance, period. A near-identical DNA to Aventus by Creed at a fraction of the price. Available in 105ml and 200ml EDT. Margin potential: 50–70% at retail. Read our full Armaf deep-dive.

02
Lattafa Khamrah

A luxurious oriental EDP with deep oud, vanilla, and woody notes. Massively popular in Saudi Arabia and the UAE. Sells particularly well in the 100ml format during Ramadan. Margin: 45–60%.

03
Al Haramain Amber Oud Gold Edition

One of Al Haramain's iconic bottles that has become a gift staple in the Gulf. The gold bottle is recognizable and sells itself as a display piece. Strong margin at 55%+.

04
Rasasi Hawas for Him

Aquatic, fresh, and long-lasting. Hawas punches way above its price point and is a consistent top-3 seller for male fragrance counters across Dubai and Sharjah. Excellent for gift sets.

05
Lattafa Oud For Glory

Bold, rich, smoky oud with a modern edge. Sells in both the UAE and Saudi markets equally well. High repurchase rate among oud lovers who want quality at an accessible price.

06
Fragrance World Supremacy Not For Everyone

A Creed Aventus-inspired composition that rivals Armaf CDNI for the top spot in the alternatives category. Extremely popular with social-media-savvy buyers aged 18–30.

07
Ard Al Zaafaran Bade'e Al Oud Oud Al Arabi

An intensely rich oriental composition with tremendous staying power. A gift-giving favourite in the wider Arab community and a strong Ramadan performer.

08
Swiss Arabian Shaghaf Oud Aswad

Deep, smoked oud with rose and leather. Swiss Arabian consistently over-performs in Emirati and Saudi consumer segments. The Aswad variant is the current bestseller in the line.

Category 2 — Designer Classics (Lower Margin, High Trust)

These fragrances don't deliver the margins of the alternatives segment, but they are essential for credibility. Customers who see your shelf stocked with genuine designer fragrances trust the rest of your range. Stock the top 5–8 at all times, focusing on the male-skewing bestsellers that dominate the UAE market.

09
Dior Sauvage EDP

The world's best-selling men's fragrance, full stop. UAE expat and tourist buyers specifically look for it. The EDP version outsells the EDT. Margin: 15–25%. Stock the 60ml and 100ml.

10
Chanel Bleu de Chanel EDP

The elegant powerhouse of the male designer segment in the UAE. Consistent year-round seller with strong demand from both locals and the large South Asian expat community.

11
YSL L'Homme / YSL Y EDP

YSL's male lineup is extremely strong in the UAE. L'Homme is the legacy, but Y EDP has overtaken it in the under-35 demographic. Stock both for maximum coverage.

12
Giorgio Armani Acqua di Giò Profumo

The Profumo version of the classic Acqua di Giò remains a consistent top-10 seller in UAE retail. Particularly strong with the 30+ male demographic.

13
Versace Eros EDP

Popular with younger UAE consumers who want a recognizable luxury name at a more accessible price point. The Eros EDP (not EDT) version is now the dominant seller.

14
Lancôme La Vie Est Belle

The top women's designer fragrance in the UAE by sales volume. Sweet, gourmand, and universally loved. Repurchase rate is high — women who discover it tend to stay loyal.

15
Paco Rabanne 1 Million

The gold brick bottle is immediately recognizable and a consistent seller across gift seasons. The lucky edition variants also move well in Q4 leading into National Day gifting.

Category 3 — Niche Risers (Lower Volume, Maximum Margin)

These fragrances are for retailers with customers who know their perfumes. The niche segment is growing fast in the UAE, driven by social media and a younger, more fragrance-literate generation of buyers. Margins of 40–80% are achievable if you build the right clientele.

16
Baccarat Rouge 540 by Maison Francis Kurkdjian

The fragrance that started the niche revolution in the Gulf. Still commanding a premium, still on every serious enthusiast's wish list. The Extrait de Parfum version is equally coveted.

17
Tom Ford Oud Wood

Tom Ford's iconic Private Blend oud-woody composition resonates strongly in the UAE where oud is culturally significant. A crossover niche fragrance that attracts both Western and local buyers.

18
Creed Aventus

The fragrance every serious UAE collector eventually owns. Creed is aspirational and high-ticket, but movement is consistent and the conversations it starts on your shop floor are priceless for upselling.

19
Initio Parfums — Oud for Greatness

The fastest-rising niche brand in the UAE market over the past two years. Oud for Greatness and Atomic Rose are the two hero SKUs. Strong word-of-mouth from fragrance social media communities.

20
Xerjoff — Naxos / Erba Pura

Xerjoff is gaining rapid traction with UAE consumers willing to spend AED 1,000+ per bottle. Erba Pura has a large following online; Naxos is the connoisseur's pick.

Honourable Mentions — Round Out Your Shelf

  • Ajmal Evoke Gold: affordable luxury for the South Asian market segment, strong in Dubai, Sharjah, and Ajman.
  • Arabian Oud Kalemat: One of the most recognizable Oriental gift fragrances in the GCC. Every retail shelf should have it.
  • Rasasi La Yuqawam: Deeply oriental, deeply UAE. A consistent seller particularly in the Northern Emirates.
  • Lattafa Asad: A newer release from Lattafa that has quickly become a social-media favourite. Lion-bottle packaging makes it highly giftable.
  • Carolina Herrera Good Girl: The top women's fragrance in the 25–40 age group in the UAE. Black stiletto bottle is instantly recognizable.

What to Stock First

If you are opening a new retail counter or refreshing your range, start with this core kit: Armaf Club De Nuit Intense Man, Lattafa Khamrah, Rasasi Hawas, Al Haramain Amber Oud, Dior Sauvage EDP, Chanel Bleu de Chanel, YSL Y EDP, and Arabian Oud Kalemat. These eight fragrances together cover male, female, Oriental, fresh, designer, and accessible categories — and every single one has a proven track record of movement in UAE retail. Add your niche picks once you've established cash flow.
